# ISO 24617-2 / DiAML

## What It Is

ISO standard 24617-2 ("Semantic annotation framework, Part 2: Dialogue acts"), with its markup
language DiAML. An application-independent, empirically and theoretically founded scheme for
annotating dialogue acts, developed primarily by Harry Bunt and colleagues, building on the
DIT++ taxonomy and the DAMSL lineage.

## Significance

The principled source for the atos speech-act taxonomy. Its general-purpose communicative
functions (Inform, Question, Request, Suggestion, Offer, Promise, Agreement/Disagreement…) and
Social Obligations functions (Greeting, Apology, Thanking, Valediction) form the v1 label set.
Its "functional segment" unit grounds the span-level approach. Shown usable for automatic
token-based annotation (per-dimension F up to ~98).

## Key Facts

- 9 semantic dimensions; functions are general-purpose or dimension-specific.
- Qualifiers: certainty, conditionality, sentiment.
- Includes rhetorical relations between dialogue units (links to RST).
- Serialized as DiAML (XML) with information-state-update semantics.
- atos v1 uses the Task + Social-Obligations functions only (drops turn/time/feedback dimensions).
