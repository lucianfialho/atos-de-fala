# TinyStories Dataset

## What It Is

A synthetic dataset of short stories written at a vocabulary level appropriate for 3-4 year old children. Generated by GPT-3.5 and GPT-4 and published on HuggingFace as `roneneldan/TinyStories`. Introduced in the 2023 paper "TinyStories: How Small Can Language Models Be and Still Speak Coherent English?" (219+ citations).

## Significance

The key insight of TinyStories: small language models failed partly because they were trained on too much data covering too many topics. By restricting the domain to simple children's stories, you can train a much smaller model that still learns correct English grammar and coherent narrative structure. This makes it the ideal dataset for a 15M parameter SLM.

The dataset covers the full range of English grammar (subject-verb-object, pronouns, tenses, conditionals) despite using limited vocabulary — because children's stories must be grammatically correct even if simple.

## Key Facts

- **HuggingFace ID:** `roneneldan/TinyStories`
- **Train split:** 2.12 million stories
- **Validation split:** ~22,000 stories
- **Generation model:** GPT-3.5 and GPT-4
- **Target audience:** Stories for 3-4 year old children
- **Format:** One story per row, text field
- **Approximate tokens:** ~100M in training split (rough estimate: 50 words/story × 2M stories × ~1 token/word)
- **Storage format in this project:** `train.bin` and `validation.bin` as uint16 memory-mapped numpy arrays
