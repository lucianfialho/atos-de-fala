# Noam Chomsky

## What It Is

American linguist and philosopher (b. 1928), founder of modern theoretical linguistics. The
project is named after him — a nod to the ambition of studying language scientifically — though
its methods and target belong to the tradition he excluded from core linguistics.

## Significance

Established that language is a window into the mind, via Universal Grammar and generative grammar.
For atos (this project), the connection is nominal: the model does pragmatics (speaker intent)
with corpus-trained statistical methods — exactly the side and the methodology Chomsky bracketed
off / criticized.

## Key Facts

- *Syntactic Structures* (1957): founded generative grammar.
- *Aspects of the Theory of Syntax* (1965): the competence/performance distinction.
- Universal Grammar: syntactic knowledge partly innate ("poverty of the stimulus").
- Criticized statistical NLP as engineering without insight; challenged by Norvig (2011).
